Complete Blood Count (CBC) Test

The Complete Blood Count (CBC) test is a comprehensive blood test that evaluates the overall health and detects a variety of disorders, including anemia, infections, and other hematological conditions. At Hindustan Laboratory, we provide accurate and reliable CBC testing using advanced technology.

What is a CBC Test?

A CBC test measures several components of your blood, including red blood cells, white blood cells, hemoglobin, hematocrit, and platelets. It provides critical information about your health and helps diagnose conditions affecting your blood or bone marrow.

Purpose of the CBC Test

  • Diagnose conditions like anemia, infections, or leukemia.
  • Monitor existing medical conditions or treatments (e.g., chemotherapy).
  • Assess overall health during routine checkups.
  • Identify abnormalities in blood cell counts.

Components of a CBC Test

Component Description Normal Range (Approx.)
Red Blood Cells (RBC) Carries oxygen from the lungs to the body. Men: 4.5–5.9 million/µL
Women: 4.1–5.1 million/µL
White Blood Cells (WBC) Fights infections and supports immunity. 4,500–11,000/µL
Hemoglobin (Hb) Protein in RBCs that carries oxygen. Men: 13.5–17.5 g/dL
Women: 12.0–15.5 g/dL
Hematocrit (Hct) Percentage of blood volume made up of RBCs. Men: 41–50%
Women: 36–44%
Platelets (PLT) Helps with blood clotting. 150,000–450,000/µL
Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV) Average size of RBCs. 80–100 fL

Preparation for the CBC Test

Generally, no special preparation is required for a CBC test. However, follow these guidelines:

  • Inform your doctor about any medications or supplements you are taking, as some may affect results.
  • Follow any specific instructions provided by Hindustan Laboratory, such as fasting (rarely required).
  • Stay hydrated to ensure an easier blood draw.

Procedure

The CBC test is a simple and quick procedure:

  1. A healthcare professional will clean the site (usually your arm) with an antiseptic.
  2. A needle is inserted into a vein to draw a small blood sample.
  3. The sample is collected in a tube and sent to our advanced laboratory for analysis.
  4. The procedure takes less than 5 minutes and involves minimal discomfort.

Interpreting CBC Results

Your CBC results will be compared to standard reference ranges. Abnormal results may indicate:

  • Low RBC/Hemoglobin: Anemia or blood loss.
  • High WBC: Infection, inflammation, or leukemia.
  • Low Platelets: Bleeding disorders or bone marrow issues.
  • High Platelets: Inflammatory conditions or certain cancers.

Always consult your doctor to interpret results, as they will consider your medical history and symptoms.
